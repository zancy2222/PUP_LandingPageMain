Vision & Mission

PUP: The National Polytechnic University

Ensuring inclusive and equitable quality education and promoting lifelong learning opportunities through a re-engineered polytechnic university by committing to:

  • Provide democratized access to educational opportunities for the holistic development of individuals with a global perspective.

  • Offer industry-oriented curricula that produce highly skilled professionals with managerial and technical capabilities and a strong sense of public service for nation building

  • Embed a culture of research and innovation

  • Continuously develop faculty and employees with the highest level of professionalism

  • Engage public and private institutions and other stakeholders for the attainment of social development goal

  • Establish a strong presence and impact in the international academic community

PUP HISTORY The PUP Quezon City Campus, previously known as PUP Commonwealth Campus, was transferred to the Polytechnic University of the Philippines in 1997 by the Housing and Urban Development Coordinating Council-National Government Center Housing Committee (HUDCC-NGCHC) and the Home Insurance And Guaranty Corporation (HIGC). The objective was to provide quality education and subsidized tuition fees to the urban poor communities of Quezon City. PUP Quezon City offers undergraduate programs in Information Technology, Business Administration, Entrepreneurship, Public Administration, and Business Teacher and Livelihood Education. It also offers a Diploma in Office Management Technology with a specialization in Medical Office Management and graduate degrees in Educational Management, Information Technology, and Public Administration. Two halls were donated to PUP for its purposes by the Sentro sa Ikauunlad ng Kasanayan at Hanapbuhay (SIKHAY) in 1997. The PUP Quezon City Campus has four buildings, including an Academic Building with 12 classrooms, an Audio-Visual Room, a Computer Laboratory, an Ecumenical Chapel, and the Winnie Castelo Welfare Complex, which is a covered court for nonacademic needs. It is considered a miniature of the PUP Main Campus in its design and is known as the "Green Campus." The PUP Quezon City Campus Act was signed into law on June 25, 2019, affirming the university's commitment to democratize access to education for economically challenged students. The campus was created through petitions and negotiations with community organizations, church groups, and government agencies. Its mission is to provide quality education to the urban poor communities of Quezon City and neighboring localities.
